# The Metacognition Playbook

A working model for deliberate AI use, grounded in 31+ studies on how AI changes the way we think.

🌐 **Live site:** [ai-and-protecting-cognition.mtmapps.now](https://ai-and-protecting-cognition.mtmapps.now/)

## What this is

A research-grounded framework for distinguishing AI use that makes you sharper from AI use that makes you softer. Built for nonprofit professionals, knowledge workers, and anyone configuring a Personal AI.

The framework:
- **2 Diagnostic Questions** — Am I trying to learn or finish? Will I catch it if it's wrong?
- **4 Design Factors** — Order of Exposure, Frontier Awareness, Effort Relocation, Configuration
- **7 Modes of AI Use** — from Lookup and Autopilot to Stewardship, Sparring Partner, and Hands Off
- **8 Cognitive Indicators of Compromise** — early warning signs you've drifted into a decline mode
- **Mode Contracts** — how to declare in advance what kind of help you want

## April 2026 Update

A second research pass added six new findings published since the original synthesis:

- **Gerber et al. (Explanation Gate, N=78)** — Cut AI-blackout failure from 77% to 39% with a single forcing function. Strongest 2026 evidence for the engaged/outsourced divide.
- **CHI 2026 Timing Study (N=393)** — Added a new principle: *form your own view first, then spar*. Active engagement is necessary but not sufficient.
- **Tian & Zhang (N=698)** — Refined Factor 4 (Configuration) with the "double-edge" mechanism.
- **Loble & Lodge** — Sharpened Mode 7 (Hands Off) with the extraneous-vs-intrinsic load distinction.
- **Bedard et al. (N≈1,500)** — "Brain fry" counter-evidence: dose matters even for engaged users.
- **Sourati et al.** — Population-scale homogenization, flagged as outside the framework's scope.

See the [April 2026 Update section](https://ai-and-protecting-cognition.mtmapps.now/#update-2026) on the live site for full detail.
